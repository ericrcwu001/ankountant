## 1. What the file is (TL;DR)

`60.pdf.pdf` is **not** a 60-item question bank. It is the complete
**Wiley CPAexcel Exam Review 2014 Study Guide — Financial Accounting and
Reporting** (author O. Ray Whittington), a **1,216-page** FAR textbook exported
to PDF via calibre. (The "60" in the filename is spurious — probably a download
counter, not a count of simulations.)

| Property         | Value                                                                             |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Title (metadata) | _Wiley CPAexcel Exam Review 2014 Study Guide, Financial Accounting and Reporting_ |
| Author           | Whittington, O. Ray                                                               |
| Pages            | 1,216                                                                             |
| Producer         | Acrobat Distiller 8.0.0 → calibre 0.9.42                                          |
| Extracted text   | ~4.0 MB (`/tmp/sixty.txt`)                                                        |
| Bookmarks (TOC)  | 361 entries, fully structured                                                     |

**Why it's valuable for us:** it is organized into **14 FAR modules** (numbered
9–22), and _nearly every module/subsection ends with a `Simulations` + `Simulation
Solutions` block_. Those blocks are **task-based simulations with full answer
keys and explanations** — exactly the raw material for our TBS surfaces. Three
appendices contain **authentic AICPA-released content** (a released FAR testlet
and the 2013 released questions), including the exact **research** answer format
and two **document-review-style** option-matching TBS.

**Vintage caveat:** 2014 content = pre-ASU-2014-09 (old revenue rules), old
lease model (ASC 840, not 842), "extraordinary items" still exist, and the exam
was 3×30 MCQ + one 7-TBS testlet. Treatments are still ~90% valid for concept
practice, but **verify any citation/treatment against current GAAP before it
ships as a graded seed** (see §9).

## 2. TBS shapes in this book & how they are graded

The book (Ch. 2 "Task-Based Simulation Grading", Ch. 3 "The Solutions
Approach", Ch. 5 SSOs) states the grading model precisely — and it matches our
step-based grader (`grading.rs`) almost exactly:

> "**All of the responses to the simulations are computer graded.** They will
> typically involve **checking a box, selecting a response from a list, or
> dragging and dropping an answer.** … no consideration is given to any comments
> or explanations outside of the structured responses."
>
> Response types: _pull-down selection, numeric/monetary inputs, formula
> answers, check-box, spreadsheet entries, **research results**._ Passing = 75%;
> partial credit via relative weightings.

So every item here is **objective and step-decomposable** → each cell/blank/line
= one `{id, answer_key, weight, tolerance}` step. Seven shapes appear:

| #  | Shape (this book)                                                                             | Response mechanic                           | Maps to our `tbs_type`      | Encodes cleanly?             |
| -- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| S1 | **Single-list matching / classification** ("select the best classification A–L for each row") | 1 dropdown per row                          | `doc_review`                | ✅ ideal                     |
| S2 | **Two-list matching** ("List A type + List B treatment per row")                              | 2 dropdowns per row                         | `doc_review` (2 blanks/row) | ✅ ideal                     |
| S3 | **Binary grid** (True/False, Yes/No, Included/Excluded, Capitalize/Expense, Accrual/Deferral) | 1 toggle per row                            | `doc_review` (2-option)     | ✅ ideal                     |
| S4 | **Research** ("Which section of the Professional Standards…? Enter the citation")             | 4 citation boxes (Topic-Subtopic-Section-¶) | `research`                  | ✅ ideal                     |
| S5 | **Numeric / calculation** ("determine the amounts below")                                     | numeric cells                               | `numeric`                   | ✅ (already shipped)         |
| S6 | **Journal entries** (select account + Dr/Cr + amount)                                         | account dropdown + numeric                  | `journal_entry`             | ✅ (already shipped)         |
| S7 | **Spreadsheet / worksheet** (trial-balance conversion, PP&E rollforward, amortization table)  | many linked numeric cells                   | `numeric` (multi-cell)      | ⚠️ large; encodable but noisy |

**S1–S3 are the ancestors of the modern document-review "which treatment
applies?" blank.** S4 is our research surface verbatim. This single source
therefore seeds **both** deferred surfaces.

## 4. Authentic AICPA document-review-style items (transcribed in full)

These four are the closest matches to our **document-review** shape

two of them are **AICPA-released**, so they are the highest-fidelity seeds.

### 4.1 ★ App B, TBS 1 — "Treatments" (AICPA-released, two-list matching)

**Directions (verbatim):** "In the table below, determine which **description**
applies to each event … and … the appropriate **accounting treatment** for each
event … Assume that all events are material in amount. A selected item may be
used once, more than once, or not at all."

**Option list — Description (col 1):** Change in accounting principle · Change in
accounting estimate · Correction of error · Neither a change nor an error
correction.
**Option list — Accounting treatment (col 2):** Current income · Cumulative
effect in current income · Prospective treatment · Retrospective adjustment ·
Restatement of previous periods · No impact.

| Event                                                                      | Description (key)                        | Treatment (key)           |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Change in LT construction contract from % -completion → completed-contract | Change in accounting principle           | Retrospective application |
| Write-off of uncollectible receivables under **direct write-off** method   | Change in accounting estimate            | Current income            |
| Write-off of uncollectible receivables under the **reserve** method        | Neither a change nor an error correction | No impact                 |
| Change in salvage value of a depreciable asset                             | Change in accounting estimate            | Current income            |
| Change in useful life of a patent                                          | Change in accounting estimate            | Current income            |

_Basis: ASC 250 (FAS 154)._ 5 events × 2 blanks = **10 gradable steps**.

## 5. Research simulations (transcribed in full)

### 5.1 The exact shape & answer format

Every research TBS uses one template:

> "Assume that you are assigned to the audit of **[Company]**. [1-sentence
> situation]. **Which section of the Professional Standards provides guidance on
> [issue]?** Enter your response in the answer fields below."

The answer is a **FASB ASC citation entered as 4 boxes**: `Topic – Subtopic –
Section – Paragraph`. The AICPA-released items pin the format:

- App B TBS5: _"What constitutes the cumulative effect of an accounting change?"_
  → **ASC 250-10-05-1**
- App C TBS3 (verbatim): _"…Blango determined the functional currency should be
  the euro. Which section of the authoritative guidance best states whether
  Blango must restate its prior-year financial statements…?"_ Answer field label:
  _"Correctly formatted FASB ASC paragraphs are 1, 2, or 3 digits followed in
  some cases by an upper case letter … do not include subparagraphs denoted by a
  lower case letter."_ → **FASB ASC 830-10-45-7**
- App A TBS7 (verbatim): _"Worthy Corporation has self-constructed an asset for
  operations. Research the professional literature to determine what types of
  assets qualify for capitalization of interest. Place the citation … in the
  answer box."_ → **ASC 835-20-15-5**

This is **our `research` surface exactly** (`PRD-tbs-shapes-future.md` T1): submit
a normalized citation string, graded against accepted citation(s), time recorded.

## 6. How to encode each shape in `exhibits_json` / `steps_json`

Our authoring JSON (in `seed_content.json`) uses `kind` → `tbs_type`,
`exhibits` → `exhibits_json` (`[{title, body}]`), and `steps` → `steps_json`.
The Rust grader (`grading.rs`) matches per step: **object** answer_key → match
each sub-field (JE lines); **scalar** answer_key → numeric (with `tolerance`) or
text match. Strings fall back to `text_matches`, so enum/citation answers work
out of the box.

### 6.1 Research (`tbs_type: "research"`) — NEW

One step, scalar string answer_key = the canonical citation. Carry accepted
variants either as a `|`-joined string or (preferred) extend the grader to read
an `accepted: []` array (small T1/T4 change). Corpus excerpt goes in `exhibits`.

```json
{
    "kind": "research",
    "prompt": "You are auditing Heath Corporation, which is considering changing its method of costing inventory. Which section of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification provides guidance on the appropriate methods for determining inventory cost? Enter the citation.",
    "set_id": "inventory_cost_methods",
    "ds_tag": "ds::inventory::cost_method",
    "exhibits": [
        {
            "title": "ASC 330-10-30 (Inventory — Initial Measurement) [excerpt]",
            "body": "…cost for inventory purposes may be determined under any one of several assumptions as to the flow of cost factors (e.g., FIFO, average, LIFO)…"
        }
    ],
    "steps": [
        {
            "id": "cite",
            "weight": 1.0,
            "label": "Governing citation",
            "answer_key": "ASC 330-10-30-9"
        }
    ],
    "source": "Wiley CPAexcel 2014 FAR, Module 10 Simulations, TBS 11 (Research)"
}
```

_Grading:_ `scalar_matches` runs `text_matches` on the citation string. Time-to-
submit is captured by the surface (T1 AC2), not the key.

### 6.2 Document-review, single-list (`tbs_type: "doc_review"`) — NEW

One step per blank; scalar **string** answer_key = the correct option label/text;
the **candidate option list** must be rendered but NOT leaked as the key. Today
`parseSteps` only reads `{id,label,weight}` and `parseExhibits` reads
`{title,body}`, so put the shared option list in an exhibit (or add a small
`options`/`choices` field the doc-review renderer reads). Answer with the option
**text**, not the letter, so the grader's `text_matches` is unambiguous and the
UI can shuffle choices (label-stripped per SPOV-4).

```json
{
    "kind": "doc_review",
    "prompt": "For each event at JIS Machinery Co. (all material, year 1), select the required accounting treatment. See the exhibit for the candidate treatments.",
    "set_id": "contingency_treatment",
    "ds_tag": "ds::contingency::accrual",
    "exhibits": [
        {
            "title": "Candidate treatments",
            "body": "Accrue and disclose | Disclose only | No accrual and no disclosure"
        }
    ],
    "steps": [
        {
            "id": "b1",
            "label": "Sued $2M; loss remote",
            "answer_key": "No accrual and no disclosure"
        },
        {
            "id": "b2",
            "label": "Sued $1.5M; reasonably possible, $200k-$500k",
            "answer_key": "Disclose only"
        },
        {
            "id": "b3",
            "label": "Fined $900k; probable, reduced to $200k-$500k, no best estimate",
            "answer_key": "Accrue and disclose"
        }
    ],
    "source": "AICPA 2013 Released FAR — TBS 1 (Contingent Liabilities)"
}
```

For the **numeric column** of that same item (accrual amount), add numeric steps
with `tolerance` (e.g. `{ "id":"b3amt", "answer_key":200000, "tolerance":1 }`) —
`doc_review` items can mix enum + numeric steps because the grader keys off the
answer_key type per step.

## 9. Caveats & data-quality notes

1. **2014 vintage — verify before shipping graded items.** Old lease model
   (ASC 840 vs 842 — item #10), pre-ASC-606 revenue, "extraordinary items"
   still exist (M9-D TBS9 option E, App-B/C references). Concept/discrimination
   value survives; **citations and some treatments need a current-GAAP pass.**
2. **IFRS mixed in.** Several released MCQ (App C) and module content are IFRS
   (revaluation model, LCNRV, one-step goodwill). Keep US-GAAP vs IFRS separate
   when tagging confusion sets.
3. **Research DB naming.** The book says "Professional Standards"; the modern
   FAR research DB is the **FASB ASC** only. Prompts should say "FASB Accounting
   Standards Codification."
4. **Table extraction fidelity.** Radio-button matrices extract as long runs of
   `○`/`●` glyphs; I recovered keys by aligning solution `●` positions with the
   option lists. Numeric worksheets (S7) and a few multi-column tables are noisy
   in text — **read the PDF page directly** (page numbers in §3) when
   transcribing those verbatim. All items in §4–§5 were cross-checked against the
   solution explanations.
5. **Licensing (R1/OQ-1).** ASC excerpt text for the research corpus must be
   scoped/verified before any public release; local seed/demo use is fine.
6. **Governmental & NFP** modules (21–22) are MCQ-only here — no sims to mine.
