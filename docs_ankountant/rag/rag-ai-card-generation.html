What we built, why, and what we skipped

What we built

A build-time retrieval-augmented pipeline (tools/cardgen/, isolated from the app) that grounds every CPA card in a named source: hybrid retrieval + rerank, gpt-5-mini generation constrained to the retrieved passage, an independent 3-bucket judge, and leakage/dedup gates — emitted as ordinary Anki notes (.apkg), each carrying its source_passage, citation, gen_method, and checker_status.

Why

Two rules drive it: provenance or it doesn't count and a wrong fact is worse than no card. Grounding makes every card traceable; the independent judge blocks wrong cards before a student sees them; the A/B/C proves hybrid retrieval beats plain keyword/vector. It runs at build time only, so the app scores with AI switched off and cards stay sync-safe (no new tables).

What we skipped

No runtime/live card generation, chatbot grading, or model-driven scoring; grading stays deterministic. Optional Learning Feedback may call OpenAI as tutoring feedback with a user key, but it does not decide correctness, scores, readiness, or scheduling. This is a bounded 300-card proof across 5 sections — BAR and the full 50k bank are deferred (machinery built, corpus not yet), and generated Tier-B cards are personal-use, not redistributable.

Generation LLM cost

--mode template expands curated templates × source-pinned data into the same Candidate schema, reusing self-check → judge → leakage → dedup → emit. Harvested straight from the ingested corpus: 77 NIST SP 800-53 controls + 34 AU-C references (identify the citation from a redacted requirement), and 201 IRS dollar-threshold cloze cards across 13 pubs. Every value traces to a verbatim source sentence (grounding verified against the ingest). A precision filter — tuned against the judge's own tmpl3 labels — keeps a cloze only when it's a rule tied to a named provision (e.g. "section 179", "adoption credit") and rejects worked-examples/dates/worksheet-refs/formulas (87% recall, 89% of bad dropped); the independent judge stays the final gate and caught 1 inverted AU-C exhibit ("not obligated to search…") that did not ship.

Honest caveats & next steps

325 shipped from a 501 pool (cloze ship-rate 29% → ~54% after the filter). Across 501 grounded cards only 1 was wrong, and the gate blocked it — the safety property templates buy. The corpus, not the filter, is now the constraint: raising per-source caps adds no cloze cards, and the ~231 genuinely-good threshold sentences in these pubs are ~87% shipped. Materially more cards means more corpus/seams (FAR via OpenStax, NIST control enhancements), not more cloze squeezing.
